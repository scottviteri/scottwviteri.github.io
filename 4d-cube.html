Mathematics (LaTeX)

The 4D rotation group SO(4) is generated by 6 planes: pairs from {1,2,3,4} (user labels 1-4, internal 0-3).

A rotation in plane \((i,j)\) by angle \(\theta\) is the block matrix

\[ R_{ij}(\theta) = \begin{pmatrix} \cos\theta & -\sin\theta \\ \sin\theta & \cos\theta \end{pmatrix} \] acting on coordinates \(i,j\) (identity elsewhere).

Projection & Depth

For a projection matrix \(P : \mathbb{R}^4 \to \mathbb{R}^2\) and point \(v\):
• 2D position = \( Pv \)
• Depth = \( \sqrt{ \|v\|^2 - \|Pv\|^2 } \) (distance to the 2D viewing plane).
Encoded as color: \(\text{hsl}(240 \cdot (1 - \text{depth}/2), 85\%, 48\%)\).

Coordinate-plane tiles are special: for tile \((i,j)\), depth is exactly preserved under rotations in \((i,j)\) or its orthogonal complement.

Clifford pairs (two disjoint planes) give isoclinic rotations (left or right depending on relative signs).
